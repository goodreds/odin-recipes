Korean Fried Cauliflower

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds cauliflower (about 1 large head)
  • canola or peanut oil (for frying)

For the batter

  • 1/2 cup potato starch
  • 10 tablespoons cake flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon white pepper
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil

For the sauce

  • 1/4 cup gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)
  • 1/4 cup ketchup
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup honey
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon minced ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon water

Directions

For the batter

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Remove the leaves from the cauliflower and cut into bite-size florets.
  3. Combine potato starch, cake flour, baking soda, salt, and white pepper in a large bowl. Add water and sesame oil; whisk until smooth.
  4. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Dip cauliflower florets in the batter and fry in batches until golden brown, 3 to 5 minutes. Transfer to the prepared baking sheet.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until crispy, about 10 minutes.

For the sauce

  1. Combine gochujang, ketchup, rice vinegar, honey, soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger in a saucepan over medium heat. Bring to a boil, stirring constantly, until sauce thickens, about 5 minutes.
  2. Combine cornstarch and water in a small bowl. Add to the saucepan and stir until sauce thickens again, about 1 minute. Remove from heat.
  3. Place cauliflower in a large bowl and pour sauce on top. Toss until well coated.
